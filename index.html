About the school

The Southern Skies Summer School is an intensive, residential program for students who want a deeper, more hands-on experience of modern astronomy. You‚Äôll spend a week learning from active researchers, working on mini-projects, and building a community that lasts long after the last observing night.

Observe the universe

Spend time at the telescope, from planning observations to calibrating and reducing your own data. Weather permitting, you‚Äôll get real photons from the night sky into your notebook.

Learn by doing

Short lectures are paired with labs in Python and Jupyter. Topics range from exoplanets and galaxies to time-domain astronomy and machine learning in astrophysics.

Meet your future peers

Connect with students from across the country and beyond. Share ideas, plan collaborations, and get a realistic picture of research life.

Accessible & supportive

We welcome applicants from diverse backgrounds and support those who are new to coding, statistics, or research environments.

Sample week

  • Day 1

    Foundations & orientation

    Welcome, intro to the campus and observatory, recap of core astronomy and statistics.

    From the HR diagram to stellar interiors, with labs using real survey data.

    Galaxies & cosmology

    Structure formation, dark matter, and mapping the large-scale universe.

  • Day 4

    Exoplanets & time-domain

    Transit and radial-velocity techniques, variable stars, and transients.

  • Day 5

    Instrumentation & data

    Detectors, telescopes, pipelines, and how big surveys are actually run.

  • Day 6‚Äì7

    Mini-projects & presentations

    Small group projects with mentoring, culminating in short presentations.

What you‚Äôll learn
